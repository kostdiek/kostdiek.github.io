Hello and Welcome!
I have recently finished my physics Ph.D. at the University of Notre Dame in South Bend, Indiana. My research was in the area of Nuclear Experiment Physics, combining Accelerator Mass Spectrometry and Gamma Ray Spectroscopy to measure the half life of the long-lived radioisotope Iron-60 for stellar and early solar system models.


On this site, you will find my current and past research projects, publications, my updated CV, a photo gallery from my research and other related travels, and my teaching philosophy statement. You will also find links to both my LinkedIn page (which as examples of conference/seminar presentations I have given) and my GitHub page (which contains some of the coding projects I have worked on). All photos (except my profile picture), designs, and coding of this website are my own work.
